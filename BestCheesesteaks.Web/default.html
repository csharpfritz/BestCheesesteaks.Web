Where are the BEST CHEESESTEAKS in Philly?

Visitors to Philly have heard a lot about the food experience here, and want to sample some of our best while they're in town. The common question that many locals get is: "Where can I get the best cheesesteak?" You an go to Pat's or Geno's, Tony Lukes, Jim's, or some of the great places in the suburbs. American? Whiz? Wit? What do you recommend? This site is build to help the out-of-town visitor to Philadelphia find the cheesesteak for them.

The Philly Cheesesteak is a simple sandwich typically served on a long Italian roll with chipped beef and cheese. Many sandwich shops offer various add-ons for your sandwich including: fried onions, peppers and broccoli rabe.

How to Order

Do yourself a favor: learn how to order a sandwich like a local. When you order a Philly Cheesesteak from a sandwich shop in town, don't make one of these common mistakes when ordering:

  • Don't call it a "Philly" or a "Philadelphia", we know what it is: a cheesesteak
  • Don't ask for it without cheese, that's almost sacrilege
  • Don't ask for "lite" or "fat free". You're ordering a cheesesteak, its got fat and it's not the healthiest. If you want healthy, try a chicken cheesesteak instead.

Do use these keywords and you'll get a bit more respect from the staff:

  • Wit Short for with Fried Onions
  • WitOut Short for without Fried Onions
  • Whiz Short for with Cheez Whiz
  • American Short for with American Cheese
... then you can mix and match these keywords and order simply:
  • Whiz Wit - A cheesesteak with cheez whiz and fried onions
  • American Witout - A cheesesteak with just American cheez and beef
